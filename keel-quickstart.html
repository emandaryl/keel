Quickstart

Build your idea. No coding required.

You bring an idea and an AI assistant. Keel does the rest — it plans your app with you in plain language, builds it, checks it works, and hands it back finished. Here's exactly how, start to finish.

You need this

An idea

Described in plain words. Like: "a website where people track how much water they drink each day."

And this

An AI assistant

Claude Code, Cursor, or Antigravity — any one works. That's the only tool to install.

The steps

Five simple steps.

1

Get Keel

Download Keel — it's a free folder of files. Think of it like a kitchen-in-a-box: the tools and the recipe card are already inside, ready to go.

2

Open it with your assistant

Point your AI assistant at the Keel folder. You don't set anything up — the folder quietly tells the assistant all the house rules.

tip: just open the folder in Claude Code, Cursor, or Antigravity
3

Tell it your idea

Type what you want in everyday words. No technical terms, no choosing tools or settings.

4

Confirm the plan

The assistant repeats your idea back in plain language and waits. Say yes, or tweak it — this is your moment to steer. It never asks you anything technical.

5

Get your finished app

Keel builds it, checks the main features actually work, and tells you exactly what to open or click. There's your app.

Good to know

A few common questions.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. That's the whole point. You describe what you want in plain words; the assistant does the building. You never touch code or settings unless you want to.

What if the app isn't quite what I meant?

At step 4 the assistant shows you its plan before building — that's your chance to fix it. And after it's built, you can just say what you'd like changed, in plain words.

What do I need to install?

Only one AI coding assistant — Claude Code, Cursor, or Antigravity. Keel itself is just a folder you open with it.

Is it really free?

Yes. Use it for personal or commercial projects, no sign-up. If it saves you time, passing it to a friend is thanks enough.
