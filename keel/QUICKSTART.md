# Build your idea with Keel

You don't need to know how to code. You need an idea and an AI coding assistant.
Keel handles everything in between.

---

## What you need
- **An idea** — described in plain words. For example: *"a website where people
  track how much water they drink each day."*
- **An AI coding assistant** — Claude Code, Cursor, or Antigravity. Any one works.

That's it. No installing tools, no picking technologies, no setup.

---

## The 5 steps

### 1. Get Keel
Download Keel — it's a free folder of files. Think of it as a kitchen-in-a-box:
all the tools and the recipe card are already inside, arranged and ready.

### 2. Open it with your assistant
Point your AI assistant at the Keel folder. You don't configure anything — the
folder quietly tells the assistant all the house rules: what to use, how to keep
things tidy, and what never to do.

### 3. Tell it your idea
Type what you want in everyday words:
> *"I want an app where people log their daily water intake and see a weekly chart."*

No technical terms needed.

### 4. Confirm the plan
Before building, the assistant repeats your idea back in plain language:
> *"So I'll make a page where people sign up, log their water each day, and see a
> chart of their week — sound right?"*

Say yes, or tell it what to change. This is where you steer. It will never ask
you anything technical — at most it asks simple choices like *"last 7 days or
last 30 on the chart?"*

### 5. Watch it build, then look
The assistant does the real work behind the scenes, following Keel's rules. When
there's something to see, it tells you exactly what to open or click — and
there's your working app.

---

## Why it works
Without Keel, the assistant has to guess how your project should be built, and it
often makes a mess you can't fix because you don't have the words for it. Keel
gives it the rules up front — so a first-timer gets a clean result, the way an
experienced developer would, without having to be one.

**Your idea + an assistant + Keel = a finished app.**
